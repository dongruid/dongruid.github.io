Research

Current Projects and Publications

Marginal Fisher Information Framework for Learning Evaluation

PhD Dissertation Research • 2024-Present

Developing a novel analytical framework based on marginal Fisher information to evaluate how much information can be extracted through learning processes. This work extends classical Fisher information theory to provide quantitative measures of learning efficiency and representational capacity.

Key contributions include comparison of different decoding strategies (oracle, dense-assumption, L1-regularized) and development of efficiency metrics for evaluating representation learning systems.

Fisher Information Information Theory Representation Learning Learning Theory

Principles of Intelligence from a Representational Perspective

Ongoing Research • 2024-2025

Investigating fundamental principles underlying intelligent systems through the lens of representation learning. This research examines how different inductive biases, particularly disentanglement, shape the way systems learn and represent knowledge.

The work connects information-theoretic foundations with practical considerations in machine learning, exploring how representational choices impact learning efficiency and generalization.

Cognitive Science Disentanglement Inductive Biases Machine Learning Theory

Dynamical Systems Analysis for Signal Processing

Research Project • 2024

Applied Dynamical Mode Decomposition (DMD) techniques to signal processing problems, exploring connections between dynamical systems theory and practical signal analysis. This work bridges theoretical mathematics with applied engineering problems.

Dynamical Systems Signal Processing Applied Mathematics DMD

Information Thermodynamics in Computing

Exploratory Research • 2024

Investigating industrial applications of information thermodynamics, with particular focus on ultra-low power computing approaches. Explored connections between thermodynamic principles and computational efficiency, including analysis of novel computing paradigms.

Information Thermodynamics Low-Power Computing Physics of Computation

Mathematical Foundations

My research draws heavily on advanced mathematical frameworks, including:

  • Differential Geometry: Manifold theory, foliations, Frobenius integrability theorem
  • Functional Analysis: Hilbert spaces, infinite-dimensional embeddings, operator theory
  • Dynamical Systems: Phase space analysis, attractor dynamics, stability theory
  • Information Theory: Fisher information, KL divergence, information geometry

Collaborations & Presentations

I regularly present research findings at seminars and reading groups. Recent presentations include work on representational perspectives on intelligence and information-theoretic approaches to learning evaluation.
